When a player selects the type of crafting from the ACTION menu 
from the main HUD, such as [actioncraftingalchemy], the 
appropriate crafting UI will be displayed.
Crafting steps:
1. Select a recipe/plan/blueprint from the list on the left.
2. Tap each “component/ingredient” slot in the middle panel. The 
appropriate items will be displayed on the right side that are 
compatible with that selected ingredient slot.
Note: Players can add exotic, rare or common compatible items for 
better outcome during crafting!
3. Tap and choose the Material item(s) from the right panel to place 
into the selected recipe slot. A check mark will appear showing it was 
accepted.
4. Choose Craft ONE or Bulk Craft.
5. (optional) Toggle on to place the items into a bulk container (if one 
is created in the player’s inventory). If no container was created or the 
current container is filled/full, a new container will be created.D

| Item Generation
Each recipe/blueprint consumes a number of raw materials (including 
upgrading). These materials can also be translated into finished goods 
that are also used within the crafting system. 
For example; Milling raw logs into usable lumber or smelting raw ore 
with flux to create ingots of pure metal. Another example is cooking 
raw meat to create roasted and editable meals.
Material substitutions
When crafting, if a recipe or blueprint requires specific material type 
(such as Ingots), the player may place any valid “Ingot” item type into 
this recipe slot. Not every recipe will allow substitutions however and 
may require explicit materials.
Example A; Bill is blacksmithing a long sword, he can place Iron ingots 
into the ingot slot or use his Adamantium ingots to craft the sword. The 
end results will vary depending on the quality of materials Bill uses.
Example B; Ken wants to mill lumber to craft bows, he can choose to 
mill his Maple logs, but decides he wants to craft powerful Etherpine
bows instead, so he begins milling his Etherpine logs with the same 
“lumber” recipe as he would use with Maple. D
Crafting with Materials 
Using Tools
Some recipes/blueprints require the use of tools to process or refine 
materials. Tools have a “Uses” durability to them before they are 
consumed and break. The quality level of the tool itself increases the 
chance of successful crafting (does not affect quality of the end 
product).
Some materials require explicit tools for crafting and making use of 
specialized workbenches, forges or other workstations. These 
requirements will be listed within the recipe/blueprint itself.
Many recipes require and consume finished goods to produce the 
desired item. An example of this is “ingots” used in nearly all 
metalworking recipes. These  are fully consumed immediately once 
the item crafting ends (either successfully or failed).

Game Summary 
Horror FPS where you explore a mansion (and its mirror dimension, accessed via VHS tapes) to 
try to fight and escape from the horrors that the mansion throws at you. 
●
Survival and Investigative horror 
●
Mirror dimension mechanic 
●
Subversive Metroidvania design 
●
Intertwining gameplay loops 
●
A Unique retro art style 
●
Minimal Interface 
The game is built on 4 design pillars: 
●
Discovery ​- It could be said that horror is less about the fear and more about the 
discovery, the searching for answers in a desolate place with the emotion of fear acting as 
a motivator - the goal is to convey the feeling of discovery throughout the game. 
●
Fear ​- The player should constantly be on edge, never feeling quite like they are safe or 
alone, and that someone is always watching, giving a constant and unending feeling of 
tension - as well as more traditional dread when desperately fighting the enemies the 
mansion throws at them while being given just enough resources to be able to have a 
feeling of... 
●
Triumph ​- when they narrowly win a battle or hit an important milestone in their escape of 
the mansion.  
●
Minimalism ​- Minimal UI Elements to aid in immersion and intentionally force the player to 
keep a close eye on their resources.  
Target Platform & Market 
Target platform is Windows PC published on Steam, Itch.io and own website.  
Target market is Males and Females between the age of 18 - 30 interested in survival horror, both 
those who want a new experience and those seeking nostalgia from the survival horror games of 
old.
